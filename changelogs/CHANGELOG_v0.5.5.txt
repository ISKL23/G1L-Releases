v0.5.5

New
- Pak mods can now be disabled and re-enabled. Toggling renames the whole
  IoStore set (.pak/.ucas/.utoc/.sig) with a ".disabled" suffix, so the
  engine stops loading it — nothing is deleted, and re-enabling is instant.
  Works from the usual drag between Enabled/Available (or double-click).
- Engine.ini tweak mods are supported (e.g. "Better Graphics", "Remove
  Grass"). G1L merges the mod's lines into your real
  %LOCALAPPDATA%\G1R\Saved\Config\Windows\Engine.ini as a clearly marked
  block. Disable/uninstall removes exactly that block: your own settings
  and other Engine.ini mods are never touched, and several such mods can
  coexist without manual merging.
- ReShade preset mods are supported. Preset .ini files are detected by
  content and installed next to the game exe (Binaries\Win64), managed
  like any other mod. ReShade itself still needs to be installed
  separately.
- Pak mods keep their folder name. An archive like
  "NH_Tattoo_Vanilla\pakchunk60-Windows_P.pak" installs into
  ~mods\NH_Tattoo_Vanilla\ and shows up as "NH_Tattoo_Vanilla" instead of
  a meaningless pakchunk name.

- Missing-dependency warnings. G1L now knows which loader each mod needs
  and warns when it's absent: BP plugins need SimpleModLoader, fyryNy
  plugins need fyryNy's Loader, ReShade presets need ReShade, Lua/DLL mods
  need UE4SS. Affected mods get a yellow warning icon with a tooltip, and
  a banner explains the first problem with an "Open mod page" button —
  no more silently dead mods.

Fixes
- Read-only files inside mod archives (some mod pages even recommend
  marking Engine.ini read-only) no longer break installation cleanup,
  toggling or deletion — the flag is cleared where needed.
- Engine.ini / ReShade mods get readable names: the Nexus download suffix
  is stripped ("Remove Grass-70-1-1780938104" → "Remove Grass").
- Pak mods in sub-folders of Content\Paks\~mods are now detected (the
  scanner previously only looked at the top level).
- Hand-installed mod paks dropped straight into Content\Paks (root) now
  show up in the launcher too. Base-game paks (G1R*, global*) and the
  SimpleModLoader core are recognised and never listed as mods.

Notes
- Disabled pak mods appear in the Available list; drag them back to
  Enabled to turn them on again.
- Engine.ini tweaks appear with a "Tweak" chip; the gear icon opens your
  actual Engine.ini for fine-tuning.
